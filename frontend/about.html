About Chess Memory

The Complete Daniel Naroditsky Chess Speedrun Database

What is Chess Memory?

Welcome to the most comprehensive Daniel Naroditsky chess speedrun database available online. This free chess opening explorer contains every position from GM Daniel Naroditsky's (affectionately known as "Danya") legendary YouTube chess speedrun series, including the famous "Speedrun to 3000," "Beginner to Master," "Sensei Speedrun," and "Master Class" series.

üéØ Our Mission: To help chess players of all levels learn from one of the world's best teachers by providing instant access to every position Danya has played, with direct links to his detailed explanations on YouTube.

Learn Chess from a Grandmaster

Daniel Naroditsky is widely regarded as one of the best chess teachers in the world. His YouTube speedrun series has helped millions of chess players improve their game by demonstrating proper chess opening theory, middlegame strategy, and endgame technique at every rating level. This interactive chess database lets you explore every position from his games, see what moves he played, what his opponents tried, and watch the corresponding YouTube video at the exact timestamp.

How to Use This Chess Opening Explorer

Simply make moves on the interactive chess board to explore different positions. The database will show you what moves Daniel Naroditsky played from that position, what his opponents tried, and link you directly to the YouTube video where you can watch Danya's explanation and analysis. It's the perfect tool for studying chess openings, understanding middlegame plans, and improving your overall chess strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Daniel Naroditsky Chess Opening Explorer?
The Daniel Naroditsky Chess Opening Explorer is a free, interactive chess database containing every position from GM Daniel Naroditsky's (Danya) famous YouTube speedrun series. It allows you to explore chess openings, see what moves Danya played, what opponents tried, and watch the corresponding YouTube videos with exact timestamps.
What chess speedrun series are included in this database?
The database includes positions from all major Daniel Naroditsky speedrun series: Back to 3000, Beginner to Master, Sensei Speedrun, Master Class, Develop Your Instincts, and Top Theory. All games are sourced from his Chess.com accounts and YouTube channel.
How do I use this chess opening explorer?
Simply make moves on the interactive chess board to explore different positions. The database will show you what moves Daniel Naroditsky played from that position, what his opponents tried, and provide direct YouTube links with timestamps to watch Danya's analysis and explanations. You can also navigate through move history and flip the board to view from either side.
Is this chess opening database free to use?
Yes, this chess opening explorer is completely free to use. No registration, subscription, or payment is required. It's an educational resource created by chess enthusiasts for the chess community to learn from GM Daniel Naroditsky's games.
Who is Daniel Naroditsky?
Daniel Naroditsky (known as "Danya") is a Chess Grandmaster and widely regarded as one of the best chess teachers in the world. His YouTube speedrun series has helped millions of chess players improve their game by demonstrating proper chess opening theory, strategy, and tactics at every rating level from beginner to master.
Can I use this on mobile devices?
Yes! This chess opening explorer is fully responsive and works great on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. You can study chess positions and explore openings from anywhere.
How is the data collected?
All game data is collected from Daniel Naroditsky's public Chess.com games through their official API. Video links are matched to corresponding YouTube videos from his channel. The database is regularly updated with new speedrun games as they're played.
How can I support this project?
This site costs roughly $10 per month to host and serve the data. If it helps your training, you can chip in via Buy Me a Coffee. Every contribution goes solely toward keeping the explorer free for the community.
